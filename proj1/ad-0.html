Albrecht Dürer's Life

Albrecht Dürer was one of Germany's most influential artists. He was born in Nuremberg in the year 1471. Dürer's connections with notable Italian artists like da Vinci and Raphael both inspired him and increased his renown. He would become one of the most influential artists of the Northern Renaissance, eventually securing patronage with Emperor Maximilian I

Dürer had wide ranging interests, including mathematical proportions of humans and sketches of wildlife. Dürer's wide body of work brought much of the Italian Renaissance back home to Germany. While Dürer is mostly known for his engravings, he also made significant contributions to biology, measurement, and many other fields.
